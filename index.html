Innovate WithIN™

Innovate WithIN™ was a project for the Indiana Department of education, which called for and mixed all of the different talents I've got. It involved a registration system, a login system, profile management, dynamic video content, and more.

The front-end of the website was built on a React.js framework with intricate database integration. The backend was built using PHP and a MySQL database, and was the sandbox in which I learned all about CRUD (Create, Read, Update, Delete). It took roughly a year to create, and is now in the hands of the IEDC.

BSU Chirper App

The Ball State University 'Chirper' is an official university app, available on both iOS and Android. The app was originally created years ago, and was used in Apple's official demo on application creation.

This reworking of the app was a complete overhaul, taking the one screen app to a multifaceted system which included the original app as well as three original games, the school's fight song, and a download-able 'sticker' package. The app now has several thousand downloads, and is used every day by BSU students and staff.

Sales & Marketing

This project for Ball State's Miller College of Business was rather interesting in nature because I came on several months into the project, when it was supposed to be done. It was my first test as a developer working with a previously built code-base.

We built a slick Wordpress / React.js article posting and resource sharing site where approved members could sign up to share and receive articles related to Sales and Marketing. This involved building custom WP plugins and bending the rules of React plugins to work for our needs. All in all, it ended in great fashion.

Clearpath [ DCP* ]

Clearpath, also known as Dual Credit Pathways, was, for me, an adventure in CSS/Javascript animations. This Wordpress site was created in just a few weeks, is completely dynamic in all of it's content. It's purpose is to allow high school students to explore collegiate dual credit options at Ball State University.

The team of developers was tasked with creating a three tiered system of categories, sub-categories, and posts, which a user could navigate through in an interactive, animated system of clicks. These circles, while they look simple, caused many a headache!
